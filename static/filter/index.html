What is FilterCloud?

A Clean, Tasteful and Powerful Loot Filter

The default FilterCloud style is designed to be close to the vanilla color scheme, in line with the dark and gritty theme of the game. No candy land rainbow puke.

It shows only items worth picking up, and uses a large font to reduce the time wasted by mis-clicks. Keep up your clearspeed, stay ahead of the game.

Customizable Style and Behaviour

A powerful style editor lets you customize the color scheme how you like it best. And you can also customize the behaviour of the filter wherever it makes sense, defining your own thresholds and fitting it perfectly to your playstyle.

Access Your Loot Filter From Anywhere

Login with your Facebook or Google account, and take your filter wherever you go. Playing at a friend's pc? Use your personal filter. Playing a quick after-work session at the office? Use your personal filter. No need to save it to Google docs or Pastebin whenever you change something. FilterCloud is always with you.

Highlighting Based on Market Value

Never miss a change in the meta, because FilterCloud highlights currency, uniques and divination cards based on their actual current market value. So if Mathil starts playing that Pledge of Hands build, you will see them highlighted while the rest of the world is still waiting for Neversink to release a new filter version.

Configure Leveling Rules Specifically For Your Build

Hand-craft a leveling filter that shows exactly the items and sockets you need for leveling. The race to endgame has never been easier!

Endgame Rares

When mapping, you want to stay in the flow and maintain your clearspeed. You don't want to stop and identify items or even take a trip back to your hideout and sort your inventory. Sure, sometimes you have really lucky drops and it can't be avoided, but most of the time, you want to pick up only those items that have a high probability of being valuable.

This filter has two tiers of rares: Highlighted rares are the base types used by currently popular builds very likely to be valuable, or take up very little inventory space like jewelry. You should always pick those up. Non-highlighted rares are still the best of their class, and have a decent chance of being sellable, but you may skip those if you have a high quantity map.

Show all fully linked rare boots, gloves and helmets, even if their basetype would normally be hidden. Rare base types that are valuable in current meta. Also includes best ring/amulet bases. Off-meta base types that may still be valuable.

Crafting

Crafting only really makes sense with items that have at least ItemLevel 84 or more, to ensure that the highest tier of affixes can roll on them. Some exceptions can be made for Atlas bases (bases with very strong implicits that only drop in certain maps) and for using level-capped Essences.

Highlight atlas bases with item level 84+. Their high implicits make them especially valuable for crafting. Even if you don't want to craft, you can usually sell these as-is for a couple chaos, some even more than one exalt. Always show map-only base types, even at lower levels. These items are always shown, even in low level areas. If you have level-capped Essences and want to use them, add the base types you're looking for here. These items are only shown if their item level is at least 84. At this item level, all possible affixes can roll on that item. These items can be rolled into very valuable uniques with an Orb of Chance (and a shit ton of luck). Per default, doesn't show league-specific ones like Leather Belt for Headhunter. If you have access to Nemesis through Zana, you may want to add that one (or replace the others).

Currency

Generally, you want to pick up all currency items because they're small and most of them have a decent value.

Low value currency like scrolls, scraps and whetstones are not worth the time picking up. You're better off maintaining a high clearspeed and buying those from other players if needed. It can still make sense to pick them up if a lot of them are close together, like after a Breach or an Artisan's Strongbox.

Uniques

Some base types have multiple uniques that can spawn on them. For example, there are five different unique Two-Stone Rings. Since the filter only sees the base type, Berek's Respite will get the same highlighting as Call of the Brotherhood, even though the former is usually worth about 400 times more than the latter.

In such cases, the filter chooses highlighting based on the most valuable unique with this base type. Some uniques are excluded, e.g. those that can only be obtained via Prophecies or Breachstones, because they can't drop anyway.

Skill Gems

Strong highlighting for very high quality gems. Highlighting for decently high quality gems. Gems with less than this will be hidden. High value gems that you should pick up regardless of quality. Medium value gems that you may also want to pick up regardless of quality.

Sockets

Six-links always receive strong highlighting, five-links always receive regular highlighting. These have a good potential of being valuable.

Six-socket items are shown without highlighting, these are good to vendor for 7 Jeweller's Orbs.

Corrupted items with white sockets are always shown. These are good for leveling gems in your off-hand.

Flasks

In endgame, your flask setup should already be optimal. Some flasks may still fetch a good price though, especially with quality. You can configure the minimum quality at which to show certain flasks.

Show Life/Mana/Hybrid flasks only if they have at least this much quality. Show Utility Flasks only if they have at least this much quality.

Jewels

Rare Jewels are very likely to be valuable. Magic jewels are usually worthless, unless both affixes are sought after in the current meta and are perfectly rolled. In that case, you may want to regal it and hope for the best.

You may also try to Alt/Augment magic jewels until you hit two good affixes. However this requires a lot of knowledge about the current meta and which affixes are valuable / possible on a certain Jewel base.

Another use for magic jewels is corrupting them and hoping for a good corrupted unique. Unless you specifically go for that though, they're not worth picking up.

Show magic jewels. Only enable this very early in the league, or when you want to try and craft a corrupted unique jewel.

Maps

Once you've reached red maps, you no longer want to pick up white ones. That's why this filter has an option to hide maps that are far below the map tier they drop in. Some yellow maps are valuable and/or worth running even if you're already doing shaped Strands, so you can define exceptions from this rule.

Tier 15/16 and unique maps are always highlighted.

Hide white maps dropping from Tier 8 maps. Hide yellow maps dropping from red maps. Tier 10 maps are always shown, and you can add your favorite maps to the exception list below. These maps are always shown, even if they would normally be hidden based on their tier.
